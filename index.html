Problem 2 – The Fibonacci Numbers

This problem tests your ability to implement (and, optionally, optimize) a simple algorithm, sanatize inputs, and further manipulate the DOM. You will need to know how to convert strings to numbers, how to remove elements from the DOM, and how to write either iterative or recursive functions.

The Fibonacci numbers fib(n) are a sequence of non-negative integers defined as follows:

  • fib(0) = 0
  • fib(1) = 1
  • fib(n) = fib(n - 1) + fib(n - 2), for n greater than one

The Fibonacci numbers are undefined for negative n.

Modify script.js such that clicking on the button below (id p2button) appends a sequence of lines/paragraphs containing fib(0) through fib(n), where n is given by the text in the text box (id p2text), to the output div (id p2output). If the specified n is negative or not an integer, instead append an error message. Subsequent clicks should refresh the contents of the output div rather than append their output to previous outputs.

Output

Warning

The simplest algorithm for the Fibonacci numbers is exponentially slow and may cause your browser to freeze or crash for n slightly above 35. As an added bonus challenge, try to optimize your solution such that this is not a problem.
